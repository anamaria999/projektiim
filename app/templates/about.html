The History Of Flower Arranging- From Ancient Times To Today: Although the history of flower arranging is long, it has only been in the last century or so that it has become an art form. Flower arranging is thought to have originated in ancient Egypt and China, where flowers were used to decorate temples and other important buildings. In more recent times, flower arrangements have been used to enhance all sorts of events, from weddings to funerals. Today, there are all sorts of different styles of flower arranging, each with its own unique set of rules. The History of Flower Meanings

The language of flowers has been recognized for centuries in many countries throughout Europe and Asia. They even play a large role in William Shakespeare’s works. Mythologies, folklore, sonnets, and plays of the ancient Greeks, Romans, Egyptians, and Chinese are peppered with flower and plant symbolism—and for good reason.

Flower Meanings by Color

Flowers provide an incredibly nuanced form of communication. Some plants, including roses, poppies, and lilies, could express a wide range of emotions based on their color alone.

What Wedding Flowers Mean

For example, look to the royal flower bouquet at the wedding of Prince William, Duke of Cambridge, to Kate Middleton (now Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge). Her all-white bouquet had lily-of-the-valley (representing trustworthiness, purity), sweet William (gallantry), hyacinth (loveliness), myrtle (love in marriage), and ivy (continuity). Altogether, these flowers’ meanings reveal the hope of a loving, everlasting marriage.
